Photosynthesis in Plants
Quick recap
  • Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants prepare their own food.
  • Chlorophyll in the leaves traps sunlight energy.
  • Carbon dioxide enters the leaf through tiny pores called stomata.
  • Water is absorbed from the soil by the roots.
  • The process produces glucose for the plant and releases oxygen.
Formula
Carbon Dioxide + Water + Sunlight ↓ Glucose + Oxygen
Memory trick

"Sun + Water + CO₂ = Glucose + O₂"

Glucose = plant food

Concept Notes
Chapter 14
Water Pollution
🌊 What is Water Pollution?
💭 Think about this...

Imagine a beautiful lake after the rainy season. The water is crystal clear.

🐟 Fish are swimming.
🦆 Birds are drinking water.
👨‍👩‍👧 Families come to enjoy the view.

Now imagine people throwing
🗑 Plastic, 🚽 Sewage, 🧪 Chemicals
into the same lake.

Would you drink that water? Probably not. That clean lake slowly becomes unsafe for fish, animals and even humans. This is called Water Pollution.

📘 Definition

Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies due to human activities. It produces physical, chemical and biological changes in water quality and makes water toxic to living organisms.

💡 Simple

When harmful substances enter water:

  • ➡️ Water quality changes.
  • ➡️ Living organisms become affected.
  • ➡️ Humans can also become sick by drinking polluted water.
🌍 Remember

Clean water supports life. Polluted water spreads disease.

⚡ One-line Summary Human activities → Pollutants → Water pollution → Harm to living organisms
🛢 Oil Spills
💭 Imagine this...

Try holding your nose for one minute.

😟 Breathing suddenly becomes difficult.

Marine animals experience something similar during an oil spill.

🌊 What happens?

When oil spreads over the sea, it forms a thin layer on the water surface. This layer:

  • ☀️ blocks sunlight
  • 💧 reduces dissolved oxygen
  • 🐠 makes it difficult for marine organisms to survive

Some organisms slowly suffocate.

📘 Textbook Point

Oil spills block sunshine, reduce dissolved oxygen and harm aquatic life.

🌍 Real-life Connection

One oil tanker accident can affect thousands of marine animals.

🧼 Household Detergents
💭 Think about this...

You finish washing your clothes. Where does that dirty water go?

🚰 Down the drain.

But the journey doesn't stop there. Many drains finally connect to rivers and lakes. The chemicals from detergents travel with the water. They slowly pollute our water bodies.

📘 Textbook Point

Synthetic detergents do not break down easily and pollute surface water and groundwater.

🔬 Microbeads
💭 Can you even see them?

Microbeads are tiny plastic particles. They're so small that you may not even notice them while using:

🧴 Face wash
🪥 Toothpaste
🧼 Shower gel
🚿 Shampoo

After washing your face... they go straight into rivers and lakes.

🐟 Then what happens?

Fish cannot tell the difference between food and plastic. They accidentally eat the microbeads. The plastic stays inside their bodies.

Later, bigger fish eat smaller fish. Eventually, these plastics move through the food chain.

📘 Textbook Point

Microbeads pollute water bodies and are accidentally eaten by aquatic organisms.

🌾 Agricultural Activities
💭 Imagine a rainy day...

A farmer has just sprayed fertilizers and pesticides.

Suddenly, 🌧 Heavy rain begins.

The rainwater carries these chemicals into nearby rivers and lakes. The water now contains too many nutrients.

Soon, 🟢 algae begin to grow rapidly. This process is called Eutrophication.

📘 Textbook Point

Fertilizers and pesticides increase nitrates and phosphates in water, causing eutrophication.

Summary

Plants use sunlight, carbon dioxide and water to produce glucose (food) and release oxygen. This process is called photosynthesis.

✎

Common exam mistake — many students write "plants make oxygen for humans." That's true, but not the main purpose. In exams, write: "The primary purpose of photosynthesis is to produce glucose for the plant. Oxygen is a by-product."

Final challenge — if I cover a plant's leaves with black paper for 3 days, will photosynthesis happen? Why?
No, because the leaves won't receive sunlight.
